# Shifting Continents

The geography of the Mesozoic was radically different from today. The movement of tectonic plates — continental drift — fundamentally shaped dinosaur evolution by connecting and isolating populations, creating new habitats, and altering climate patterns.

## Pangaea: The Supercontinent

At the start of the Triassic, nearly all of Earth's landmass was joined into a single supercontinent called **Pangaea**. This had profound consequences:

- Early dinosaurs could potentially disperse across the entire landmass
- Triassic dinosaur faunas are relatively similar worldwide
- The interior of Pangaea was extremely hot and arid — far from any ocean's moderating influence

## The Great Breakup

Pangaea began rifting apart during the Jurassic, and the process accelerated through the Cretaceous:

### Jurassic (~200–145 Mya)
- Pangaea splits into **Laurasia** (north) and **Gondwana** (south)
- The North Atlantic Ocean begins to open
- Land bridges still allow some faunal exchange

### Early Cretaceous (~145–100 Mya)
- South America and Africa begin separating
- India breaks away from Africa and begins its journey toward Asia
- The Tethys Sea expands between northern and southern landmasses

### Late Cretaceous (~100–66 Mya)
- South America becomes an island continent
- Africa drifts northward
- The Western Interior Seaway splits North America in two
- India is an isolated island, racing toward its collision with Asia

## How Continental Drift Shaped Dinosaurs

### Isolation and Divergence
As continents separated, dinosaur populations became isolated and evolved independently:
- **Tyrannosaurs** dominated in North America and Asia (connected via Beringia)
- **Abelisaurids** (like Carnotaurus) were the apex predators in South America, Africa, India, and Madagascar
- **Titanosaur sauropods** thrived on southern continents long after other sauropod families declined in the north

### Sweepstakes Dispersal
Even separated continents weren't completely isolated. Occasional over-water dispersals ("sweepstakes routes") allowed some species to colonize new landmasses:
- Hadrosaurs reached South America in the Late Cretaceous, possibly via island chains

### Endemism
Isolated landmasses developed unique faunas:
- Madagascar had bizarre species like the predator *Majungasaurus*
- European islands hosted dwarf dinosaurs adapted to limited resources

## The Western Interior Seaway

One of the most dramatic geographic features of the Cretaceous was the **Western Interior Seaway** — a vast inland sea stretching from the Gulf of Mexico to the Arctic Ocean, splitting North America into two separate landmasses from roughly 100–70 Mya. This:
- Separated eastern and western North American dinosaur faunas
- Created rich coastal habitats
- Is why most famous North American dinosaurs come from the western half of the continent

> **Key Takeaway:** Continental drift was one of the most powerful forces shaping dinosaur evolution. The breakup of Pangaea isolated populations, drove independent evolution on different continents, and created the distinct dinosaur faunas we see in different parts of the world.

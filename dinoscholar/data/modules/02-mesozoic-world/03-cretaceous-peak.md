# Cretaceous: Peak Diversity

The Cretaceous Period (145–66 Mya) was the longest and most dynamic period of the Mesozoic. Dinosaurs reached their peak diversity, flowering plants revolutionized terrestrial ecosystems, and the stage was set for the dramatic finale of the K-Pg extinction.

## A Changing World

The Cretaceous landscape would have been strikingly different from the Jurassic:

- **Flowering plants (angiosperms)** appeared in the Early Cretaceous and spread explosively. By the Late Cretaceous, they dominated many ecosystems, replacing conifers and ferns as the primary vegetation.
- This botanical revolution created new food sources and habitats, driving the diversification of herbivorous dinosaurs with specialized feeding adaptations.

## Herbivore Diversity Explodes

The Cretaceous produced an extraordinary variety of plant-eating dinosaurs:

### Hadrosaurs (Duck-Billed Dinosaurs)
- **Parasaurolophus** — With its trombone-like crest for long-distance communication
- **Edmontosaurus** — One of the most common and largest hadrosaurs
- **Corythosaurus** — Sporting a helmet-shaped resonating crest

### Ceratopsians (Horned Dinosaurs)
- **Triceratops** — The iconic three-horned giant
- **Styracosaurus** — With its spectacular spiked frill
- **Protoceratops** — A small, common ceratopsian of Mongolia

### Armoured Dinosaurs
- **Ankylosaurus** — Covered in bony plates with a devastating tail club
- **Euoplocephalus** — So well-armoured that even its eyelids had bone

### Titanosaur Sauropods
- **Argentinosaurus** — A contender for the largest land animal ever
- **Patagotitan** — A 69-tonne colossus known from relatively complete remains

## Apex Predators

The Cretaceous produced the most famous predatory dinosaurs, though different families dominated in different parts of the world:

- **Northern continents (Laurasia)**: Tyrannosaurs — T. rex, Tarbosaurus, Albertosaurus
- **Southern continents (Gondwana)**: Abelisaurids (Carnotaurus) and carcharodontosaurids (Giganotosaurus)
- **Aquatic niches**: Spinosaurids like Spinosaurus hunted fish in rivers

## Small But Significant

Not all Cretaceous dinosaurs were giants. Many small species played crucial ecological roles:

- **Velociraptor** — A feathered predator the size of a large turkey
- **Microraptor** — A four-winged glider the size of a pigeon
- **Gallimimus** — An ostrich-like omnivore built for speed

## The Eve of Extinction

By the final stage of the Cretaceous (the Maastrichtian, 72–66 Mya), dinosaur ecosystems were well-established and diverse. The Hell Creek Formation preserves a snapshot of this final world: T. rex as apex predator, Triceratops and Edmontosaurus as common herbivores, and a supporting cast of smaller species.

Then, 66 million years ago, everything changed.

> **Key Takeaway:** The Cretaceous was the peak of dinosaur evolution, with more species and greater ecological diversity than at any other time. The rise of flowering plants drove herbivore diversification, while different predator families dominated different continents — a pattern shaped by continental breakup.

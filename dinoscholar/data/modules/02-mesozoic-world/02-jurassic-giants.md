# Jurassic: Age of Giants

The Jurassic Period (201–145 Mya) is often considered the quintessential "age of dinosaurs." Freed from competition by the end-Triassic extinction, dinosaurs diversified rapidly and grew to astounding sizes. The Morrison Formation of western North America provides a vivid window into this world.

## Sauropod Supremacy

The Jurassic saw sauropod dinosaurs reach their greatest diversity and some of their largest sizes:

- **Brachiosaurus** — Standing 12 metres tall, it browsed treetops like a living crane
- **Diplodocus** — At 26 metres long, it swept its neck side to side to cover vast feeding areas
- **Apatosaurus** — A robust giant weighing as much as four elephants

These animals achieved sizes unprecedented in the history of life. Several factors enabled their gigantism:
- **Pneumatic bones** — Air-filled bones reduced weight without sacrificing strength
- **Efficient respiration** — A bird-like flow-through breathing system
- **No chewing** — Swallowing food whole eliminated the constraint of having a heavy skull on a long neck
- **Rapid growth rates** — Some sauropods gained tonnes per year during adolescence

## Theropod Predators

The Jurassic also produced formidable predators:

- **Allosaurus** — The apex predator of the Morrison Formation, up to 9.7 metres long
- **Ceratosaurus** — A horned predator that coexisted with Allosaurus
- **Saurophaganax** — Possibly the largest Jurassic predator at 13 metres

These predators had to contend with armoured prey:
- **Stegosaurus** — With its back plates and deadly thagomizer
- **Kentrosaurus** — An African stegosaur bristling with spikes

## The First Flyers

The Late Jurassic produced one of the most important fossils ever found: *Archaeopteryx lithographica*, discovered in the Solnhofen Limestone of Germany in 1861. This crow-sized animal possessed:
- Feathered wings suitable for flight or gliding
- Teeth, clawed fingers, and a bony tail (dinosaur features)
- A wishbone and flight feathers (bird features)

Archaeopteryx demonstrated that birds evolved from small theropod dinosaurs, providing a dramatic vindication of evolutionary theory just two years after Darwin's *On the Origin of Species*.

## A Global Stage

During the Jurassic, the supercontinent Pangaea continued to rift apart. Laurasia (north) and Gondwana (south) separated, but land bridges still allowed some faunal interchange. Similar dinosaur faunas are found across multiple continents — Brachiosaurus-like sauropods in both North America and East Africa, for example.

> **Key Takeaway:** The Jurassic was the era of unprecedented size, with sauropods becoming the largest land animals ever and theropod predators evolving to match. The appearance of Archaeopteryx marks the beginning of one of evolution's greatest transitions — dinosaurs to birds.

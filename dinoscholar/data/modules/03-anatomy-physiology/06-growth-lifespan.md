# Growth and Lifespan

How fast did dinosaurs grow, and how long did they live? Thanks to bone histology — the microscopic study of bone tissue — we can now answer these questions with remarkable precision.

## Reading Growth in Bone

Like trees, dinosaur bones contain annual growth markers:

- **Lines of Arrested Growth (LAGs)** — Dark lines in bone cross-sections that mark seasonal pauses in growth, like tree rings
- **Fibrolamellar bone** — Fast-growing, well-vascularized bone tissue indicating rapid growth
- **Lamellar bone** — Slow, dense bone deposited when growth decelerates
- The transition from fibrolamellar to lamellar bone marks the approach to adult size

## Growth Rates

### Tyrannosaurs
T. rex went through an explosive adolescent growth spurt:
- Relatively slow growth for the first ~10 years
- Ages 14–18: a dramatic spurt, gaining ~2 kg per day (~770 kg per year)
- Reached roughly 5,000 kg by age 18
- Growth slowed dramatically after age 20
- Maximum lifespan probably ~28–30 years

### Sauropods
Giant sauropods grew at extraordinary rates:
- Some species gained over 2 tonnes per year during peak growth
- **Apatosaurus** may have reached near-adult mass in 15–20 years
- Continued slow growth throughout life (indeterminate growth)
- Maximum age estimates range from 50–100+ years

### Small Theropods
- Smaller dinosaurs reached adult size more quickly — often within 1–3 years
- **Velociraptor** may have been fully grown by age 3–4

## Life History Patterns

Dinosaur growth patterns reveal important life history information:

| Trait | Small Theropods | Large Theropods | Sauropods |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Age at maturity | 1–3 years | 15–20 years | 15–30 years |
| Growth strategy | Fast, short | Explosive spurt | Sustained fast |
| Maximum age | 5–10 years | 25–30 years | 50–100+ years |
| Reproductive age | ~1–2 years | ~15 years | ~15–20 years |

## Mortality and Survival

Studies of large populations (like Albertosaurus bonebeds) reveal survival curves:
- High mortality in juveniles (predation, disease)
- Lower mortality in subadults and young adults
- Increasing mortality past peak reproductive age
- Patterns similar to large modern mammals

## Sexual Maturity

Some dinosaurs show evidence of reaching sexual maturity before reaching full adult size:
- **Medullary bone** (a calcium-rich bone layer produced by egg-laying females) has been found in T. rex specimens that had not yet completed growth
- This suggests reproduction began during the adolescent growth spurt — a strategy for maximizing reproductive output in a dangerous world

> **Key Takeaway:** Dinosaur bone histology reveals growth rates rivaling or exceeding those of modern mammals. Many large dinosaurs went through dramatic growth spurts during adolescence, reaching massive sizes in surprisingly short timeframes.
